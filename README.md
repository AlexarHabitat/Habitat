Habitat Archive is a de-capitalized, de-politicized repository for civilizational experiments, dedicated to documenting and exploring the ideas, plans, and practices of the New Renaissance Habitats.
It serves both as a living community operating system and a sandbox for institutional innovation.

Core Mission: To create new aesthetics, narratives, and ways of living, offering independent space for outsiders and creators.

Fundamental Principles: Nonviolence, decentralization, task collaboration, and time-autonomy welfare.

Content Structure: Includes manifestos and philosophical essays, preparation and construction plans, collaboration protocols and institutional designs, case studies and external references, as well as blog posts and academic resources.

Positioning: Each habitat remains small in scale, emphasizing internal self-sufficiency; external economy is supplementary only. Habitats do not unite as political entities but engage in cultural and academic exchanges, forming a loose civilizational network.
